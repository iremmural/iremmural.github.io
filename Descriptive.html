Descriptive Visualizations

Detailed statistical analysis of NYC motor vehicle collisions, focusing on temporal, spatial, and causal factors.

Geographic Total Collision Heatmap

When examining the safety panorama of New York City's vast transportation network, it becomes clear that accidents are not uniformly distributed; instead, they cluster within specific corridors and neighborhoods. This interactive map visualizes these clusters through a geographic lens, offering initial insights for urban safety strategies. The peak in activity, particularly during rush hours, highlights the direct link between traffic volume and collision risk across the five boroughs.

Vehicle & Factor Risk Matrix

Identifying the location of an accident is only the first step; the true objective is to evaluate the severity of the risks involved. This matrix correlates the probability of an incident with its potential physical and social impact. By categorizing street events into a structured framework, it enables the differentiation between frequent, low-impact collisions and the high-priority 'red alert' zones that demand immediate intervention due to their devastating consequences.

Urban Vulnerability Distribution

The most vital phase of this analysis centers on urban fragility. This distribution curve illustrates the varying degrees of vulnerability across different city sectors to traffic-related hazards. The peaks represent the most sensitive areas of New York, where residents face the highest exposure to danger. This visualization serves as a roadmap for building a more resilient city by pinpointing the specific 'weak links' where safety improvements are most urgently needed.
