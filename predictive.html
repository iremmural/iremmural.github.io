Predictive Visualizations

A Comprehensive Diagnostic Study of Model Reliability and Classification Metrics in Traffic Safety.

Performance Overview

The primary objective of this predictive analysis was to develop a model capable of identifying the likelihood of casualties in traffic incidents. However, initial performance metrics indicated that the model lacked sufficient discriminative power for individual event prediction. Therefore, we developed a descriptive model to explore the relationships between variables and casualty.

Casualty Risk Odds Ratios

This descriptive logistic regression model identifies the primary drivers of casualty occurrence. The most striking finding is that two-wheeled vehicles have an exceptionally high odds ratio, confirming they are the most vulnerable group in NYC traffic.

Regarding contributing factors, impairment and environmental conditions significantly increase the likelihood of a casualty. Conversely, weekends show a slightly lower relative risk compared to the weekday baseline.
