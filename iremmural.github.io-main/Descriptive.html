Descriptive Visualizations

Detailed statistical analysis of NYC motor vehicle collisions, focusing on temporal, spatial, and causal factors.

Geographic Total Collision Heatmap

This interactive map visualizes the spatial distribution of collisions across the five boroughs of New York City. Brooklyn and Manhattan show the highest geographic density of incidents, while Staten Island displays the lowest concentration. The high density in the central boroughs is a direct result of intricate road networks, higher vehicle-to-infrastructure ratios, and significant tourist foot traffic.

Vehicle & Factor Risk Matrix

This matrix displays average casualty rates for different vehicle types. Two-wheeled vehicles exhibit the highest vulnerability, especially when impairment is involved, reaching a critical casualty rate of 0.99.

Urban Vulnerability Distribution

The graph compares casualty counts for motorists, pedestrians, and cyclists across all boroughs. Brooklyn records the highest number of motorist and pedestrian casualties, while Staten Island remains the area with the lowest counts due to lower urban density.
